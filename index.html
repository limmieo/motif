Version History
How it works: The Wario Synthesis Engine analyses MIDI files to identify melody, bass, and chord tracks. It then resynthesises each part using Web Audio oscillators tuned to mimic the Game Boy's 4-channel sound chip: two pulse wave channels, one wave channel, and one noise channel. All processing runs client-side in your browser - no server-side audio generation. Built with TypeScript, Vite, and Claude Code.

WHY I MADE THIS

Why this fork exists

I love the idea of turning familiar songs and sounds into Game Boy-style versions. It adds a new creative element for videos, streams, games, and other content.

The limitation I wanted to fix

The original version mainly searched for MIDI files already available through its sources. That leaves out a lot of music. This fork adds audio uploads, YouTube-title importing, dedicated piano transcription, and smarter arrangement so creators have more options.

Creators and copyright

I wanted to explore whether a heavily reinterpreted chiptune version could be more useful for content creators than simply replaying the original recording. However, transformation does not automatically clear copyright, prevent claims, or grant permission. Credit the original artist and only use music you have the right to use.

Credit to the original

This is the Tony Destin fork of Wario Synth, built from the original project by b1rdmania. The original concept, interface, and synthesis foundation deserve full credit.
